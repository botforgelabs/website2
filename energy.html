Understanding Energy

How motion and position shape the physical world

What Is Energy?

Energy is the capacity to do work or cause change in a physical system. In physics, energy appears in many forms including motion, heat, chemical reactions, electricity, and radiation. Mechanical energy is commonly divided into two main forms: potential energy and kinetic energy.

Potential Energy

Potential energy is stored energy that depends on an object's position, arrangement, or state. For example, an object held above the ground has gravitational potential energy because gravity can pull it downward. When the object falls, that stored energy converts into motion.

Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy is the energy of motion. Any object with mass that moves has kinetic energy. The faster the object moves, or the more mass it has, the greater its kinetic energy becomes.

Conservation of Energy

One of the most important ideas in physics is the conservation of energy. Energy cannot be created or destroyed; it can only change form. Potential energy can become kinetic energy, heat energy, electrical energy, or other forms depending on the system.
