Understanding Energy

Potential Energy

Potential energy is stored energy. It exists because of an object's position or configuration. For example, a rock held high above the ground has gravitational potential energy because gravity is ready to pull it downward.

Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy is the energy of motion. Anything that moves — a car, a falling apple, or even molecules vibrating in warm air — has kinetic energy. The faster something moves and the more mass it has, the more kinetic energy it contains.

Energy Transformation

Energy constantly changes form but is never destroyed. When the rock falls, its potential energy transforms into kinetic energy. This idea is part of one of the most important principles in physics: the conservation of energy.
